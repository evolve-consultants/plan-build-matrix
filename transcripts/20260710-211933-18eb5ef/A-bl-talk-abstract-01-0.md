# Design System Talk Abstract Template

**Title options:**
- *[System Name]: Building [X] from Scratch*
- *Scaling Design Without Chaos: Our [X] Journey*
- *From Fragments to Framework: How We Built [System Name]*

**Structure:**

Open with a honest problem statement—what broke before you had the system? (A specific moment lands better than "inconsistency.")

**The middle should answer:**
- What was the bet you made? (speed, quality, scalability?)
- What surprised you most?
- One decision that shaped everything else

**Close with concrete proof:**
—not "we saved time" but *how* (metrics, what teams now do differently, what's possible now that wasn't)

---

**Tone tip:** Avoid "we built a system and it was great." Lean into what was hard or counterintuitive. Audiences want to know what you'd do differently, not a victory lap.

**Length ballpark:** 150-200 words typically works for conference submissions.

**What I need from you to tighten this:**
- What was the specific pain point?
- How far along is your system now?
- What's the most surprising thing you learned?
