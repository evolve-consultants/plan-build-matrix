# Trial Onboarding Sequence

## Email 1: Welcome + Quick Win (Day 0-1)
**Subject:** You're in—let's get you scheduled in 5 minutes

- Warm welcome, name them
- One clear CTA: "Create your first event" (link to app)
- Show a 30-second screenshot or GIF of the core flow
- Reassure them: "No credit card needed, full access for 14 days"
- Soft mention: "Most users X by day 3" (whatever your quick win is—saves 2 hours/week scheduling, etc.)
- Tip: Keep copy short. They're skeptical of onboarding emails.

---

## Email 2: Show Value (Day 3-4)
**Subject:** [Name], here's what people do with this

- Lead with usage: "You've created X events—here's what's next"
- Feature spotlight: Pick your second-biggest differentiator (if #1 was ease-of-use, maybe it's integrations or AI scheduling)
- Social proof: "Teams using [integration/feature] save X hours per week"
- CTA: Specific task ("Connect your calendar" or "Try smart scheduling on your next meeting")
- Optional: Brief troubleshooting if they haven't engaged ("Stuck? Here's our 2-min setup guide")

---

## Email 3: Upgrade Nudge (Day 10-11)
**Subject:** Your trial expires in 4 days—here's what you'd keep

- Acknowledge progress: Reference something they actually *did* in trial
- Value recap: Quantify the benefit (time saved, meetings scheduled, integrations used)
- Clear pricing/CTA: "Start free" or simple pricing table
- Objection handling: "Questions? Reply to this email" (makes it personal)
- Fallback: "Not ready? We'll keep your trial data for 30 days"
