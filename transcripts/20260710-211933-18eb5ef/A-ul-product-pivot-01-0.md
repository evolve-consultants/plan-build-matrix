# Starting Points

**First, validate there's actual demand:**
- Talk to 10-15 potential customers outside your network before building anything new
- Are they struggling with this problem? Would they pay for a solution?
- This saves you from polishing something nobody wants

**Then assess your actual starting position:**
- How much of your internal tool is genuinely reusable vs. built for your specific workflows?
- What's the delta between "works for us" and "works for a stranger with different processes"?
- This is usually bigger than people expect

**Clarify your constraints early:**
- Do you have time to build *and* support a product while running the consultancy?
- Can you stomach the shift from customization (your current model) to standardization?
- What's your funding situation—bootstrapping or raising capital?

**The hard part:**
Most consultancy tools fail as products because they're too opinionated about workflows. Customers want flexibility; product businesses need repeatability. You'll likely need to:
- Strip it down to the core value
- Add configuration/customization options
- Build onboarding for people who aren't you

**Consider the alternative:** Some founders license their tool to competitors instead of building a full product business. Lower lift, decent revenue.

What type of tooling are we talking about? That shapes whether product-ifying makes sense.
