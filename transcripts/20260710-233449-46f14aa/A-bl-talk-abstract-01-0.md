# Design System Conference Talk Abstract [Draft]

**Title:** [Your system name] — Building Design Infrastructure for [Scale/Context]

**The Hook:**
Every design system starts with a problem. Ours began when [choose one: our team couldn't ship consistently / we had seven button styles across products / communication between design and engineering broke down]. What we discovered building [System Name] was that infrastructure isn't just about components—it's about organizational alignment.

**The Body:**
We'll walk through three critical decisions that shaped our system:
- **What we standardized** (and what we didn't)
- **How we built buy-in** across teams who didn't ask for it
- **The metrics that actually mattered** (spoiler: not component adoption)

**The Payoff:**
You'll leave with a framework for: assessing whether you actually need a design system, surviving the messy middle where adoption stalls, and measuring success beyond the pretty documentation site.

**Tone notes for you to adjust:**
- Make the opening problem *specific and relatable* (not generic)
- The three points should reflect your actual journey—what surprised you?
- The payoff works best if it promises something tactical, not philosophical
