# Getting Started: Internal Tool → Product

**First, validate there's actually a market:**
- Talk to 10-15 people outside your company who do similar work. Are they struggling with this problem? Would they pay for a solution?
- Check if competitors exist (good sign: market exists; bad sign: might be hard to differentiate)
- Get rough pricing signals early—what would they pay?

**Then assess your starting position:**
- **Technical debt**: Is the internal tool actually production-ready, or would it need major rebuilding? (Be honest. Most internal tools aren't.)
- **Your advantage**: What do you know from building this for yourselves that others don't?
- **Resource commitment**: Can you realistically support a product while running your consultancy? This often requires splitting focus.

**If you clear those hurdles:**
- Pick a narrow initial customer segment (not "everyone in [industry]")
- Build a simple landing page, get 5-10 early customers paying *something*
- Figure out your support model—will you do it yourself, hire someone, or keep it minimal?

**Common pitfalls:**
- Assuming internal needs = market needs (they often don't)
- Underestimating the gap between "tool we use" and "product customers will pay for"
- Spreading too thin between consulting revenue and product development

What type of tool are we talking about, roughly? That affects whether this is realistic part-time or needs dedicated focus.
