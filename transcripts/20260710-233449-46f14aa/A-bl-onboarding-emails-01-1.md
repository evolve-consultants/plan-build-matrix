# Trial Onboarding Sequence

## Email 1: Welcome + Quick Win (Day 0-1)
**Subject:** You're in—let's schedule your first meeting

- Warm greeting, confirm trial access works
- One obvious first task: "Create a calendar and share it with one person"
- Direct link to a 2-min walkthrough video (or interactive tutorial)
- Reassure them the interface is simple
- CTA: "Set up your first shared calendar"
- Optional: "Reply with any questions—real person responds"

---

## Email 2: Show Value (Day 3-4)
**Subject:** [Name], here's what people do with this first

- Case for why they signed up (pain point callback)
- 2-3 concrete use cases: team meeting scheduling / client booking / personal time-blocking
- Which feature solves which problem (feature-benefit pairing)
- Screenshot or short clip of the most-used workflow
- Soft ask: "Which of these resonates? Let me know what you're trying to solve"

---

## Email 3: Reduce Friction (Day 7)
**Subject:** Quick tip to save you 5 minutes this week

- One power-user hack (recurring meetings / bulk import / integration tip)
- Acknowledge they might have gotten busy or hit a snag
- Low-pressure: "If you're stuck here, here's help"
- Check-in: "What's one thing you wish was easier?"
- Upgrade path mention (soft, not pushy)

---

**Notes to shape:**
- What's your primary use case? (vs. secondary ones)
- How technical are trial users?
- What's your biggest drop-off point?
- Do you have integration partners worth highlighting early?
