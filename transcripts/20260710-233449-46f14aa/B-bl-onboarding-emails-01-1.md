## Draft Sequence (Option A)

**Email 1: Day 0 (Welcome + First Action)**
- Subject line: "You're in — here's the fastest way to start"
- Body: 
  - Brief warmth (you're set up, here's what's next)
  - One specific thing to do in the next 10 min (create an event, import calendar, whatever your aha is)
  - Direct link to that feature
  - Reassure them there's no wrong way to explore
  - Signature

**Email 2: Day 3 (Show Second-Order Value)**
- Subject line: "The thing most people miss (and why it saves time)"
- Body:
  - Reference something they likely did by now (created an event)
  - Show a second feature that compounds that (sharing, recurring, integration, blocking focus time — whatever comes after the first action)
  - Social proof if you have it (X people use this to do Y)
  - Link to feature or a short guide
  - Keep it short

**Email 3: Day 10 (Convert/Extend)**
- Subject line: "Your trial ends in [X] days — here's what we didn't cover yet"
- Body:
  - Reframe the trial deadline as positive (not pressure, but clarity)
  - Offer one of:
    - A feature they haven't tried that might matter to them
    - A use case they might not have thought of
    - An upgrade incentive (discount for annual, extra features, team seat)
  - CTA is clear and low-friction (button to subscribe, not a sales call)
  - Fallback: "Not ready yet? We'll check in in a week"

---

### Why this shape
- **Email 1** gets them across the activation threshold (first success)
- **Email 2** expands their usage pattern (compounds habit)
- **Email 3** lets them opt in to next phase instead of feeling abandoned or pressured

The gaps (day 0→3, day 3→10) let them sit with the product. You're not pushing; you're prompting.

### Alternatives I considered
- **B** (Four-email sequence): Add a "day 1 success check-in" to catch people who hit friction immediately. Useful if your first action is hard or has high abandonment.
- **C** (Incentive-first): Lead email 3 with discount/offer before feature highlights. Faster conversion, but selects for price-sensitive users and leaves competence-building on the table.
