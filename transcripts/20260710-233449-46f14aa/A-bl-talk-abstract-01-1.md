# Design System from the Ground Up: What We Built and Why It Mattered

**The Setup (Problem)**
Most design systems fail silently—built in isolation, adopted reluctantly, maintained by no one. We were about to become another cautionary tale.

**The Tension (Why it's interesting)**
We discovered the real challenge wasn't design governance or component libraries. It was organizational will. How do you convince a company moving at startup speed that slowing down to build infrastructure is the right move?

**The Arc (What we did)**
We'll walk through our three-phase approach: (1) winning internal credibility through high-impact early wins, (2) scaling adoption by embedding ourselves in product teams rather than standing apart, and (3) building the right governance model—one that evolved as we learned what actually stuck.

**The Payoff (Why you should care)**
The design system is still alive three years later. More importantly, it's actively used because we built it *with* the org, not *for* it. We'll share what surprised us most about sustaining momentum, the metrics that actually matter, and the one decision we'd make differently.
