# Starting Points to Consider

**First, clarify your actual advantage:**
- Is it the tool itself, or your domain expertise? (Often it's the latter)
- Could customers use it without your ongoing support?
- What would they need to learn/change about their workflows?

**Reality check the market:**
- Who specifically would buy this? Talk to 5-10 people outside your current clients
- What are they using now, and why would they switch?
- What's the pricing model—per user? Per seat? Usage-based?
- Is this a nice-to-have or a pain point they'd budget for?

**Assess the effort:**
- How much code would need rewriting to work for other companies?
- Would you need to support multiple integrations, SSO, compliance requirements?
- What's your honest bandwidth—is this a side project or would it need dedicated focus?

**Consider the middle path:**
- Some consultancies sell their tools as a *service* (you run it for clients) rather than a product
- Or license it to a few companies without building a full product operation
- This avoids the sales/support/infrastructure burden while generating revenue

**The hard part:** Building software people will pay for is different from building software that solves *your* problem. The latter is much easier.

What type of tool are we talking about? That shapes whether this makes sense.
