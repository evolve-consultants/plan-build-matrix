# Outline: Why We Moved Away from Microservices

## Introduction
- Hook: The microservices hype vs. reality
- Thesis: Our decision to consolidate and why

## The Original Appeal
- What drew us to microservices initially
- Promises: scalability, team autonomy, independent deployments
- Context: what problem we were trying to solve

## Where It Got Complicated
- Operational overhead (monitoring, logging, debugging across services)
- Network latency and reliability issues
- Data consistency and distributed transaction challenges
- Deployment complexity increased rather than decreased

## The Hidden Costs We Didn't Anticipate
- Team coordination overhead (actually increased)
- Duplicate code and logic across services
- Testing became harder, not easier
- DevOps/infrastructure burden on small teams

## The Decision to Change Course
- What metrics/pain points triggered the reassessment
- The specific breaking point(s)
- How we evaluated alternatives

## What We Moved Toward
- Monolith vs. modular monolith vs. hybrid approach
- How we structured it differently this time
- What we kept from microservices thinking (modularity, clear boundaries)

## Lessons Learned
- When microservices make sense (and when they don't)
- Questions to ask before adopting them
- What we'd do differently

## Conclusion
- It's not about the architecture, it's about the constraints
