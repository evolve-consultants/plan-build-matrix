# Starting Points for Productizing Internal Tools

**First, be honest about the gap:**
- Internal tools are optimized for *you* — your workflows, tolerances, technical depth
- Products need to work for strangers with different setups, skill levels, and patience
- This is usually a bigger lift than founders expect

**Do basic market validation before investing heavily:**
- Who else has this exact problem? (Talk to 10-15 potential customers, don't just assume)
- Would they pay for it, or just "that would be nice"?
- What are they using now, and why aren't they satisfied?
- How big is the addressable market?

**Assess the actual work:**
- **Documentation & support** — your tool probably lacks both. This is often 50% of productization work
- **Generalization** — removing your specific assumptions, industry jargon, workflows
- **Reliability** — internal tools can have rough edges that customers won't tolerate
- **Security/compliance** — hosting for others, data handling, SLAs
- **Pricing model** — does this work as SaaS, one-time license, or something else?

**Practical next step:**
Pick 2-3 potential customers and let them try your tool as-is. Watch where they struggle. That friction is real product development work.

**Real talk:** Many consultancies stay internal-tool-only because they're profitable without the distraction. Is this about revenue growth, or solving a specific market problem? The answer shapes everything.
