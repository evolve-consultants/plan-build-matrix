# 3-Email Trial Onboarding Sequence

## Email 1: Welcome + Quick Win (Day 0-1)
**Subject:** Get your first event scheduled in 2 minutes

- Brief welcome, acknowledge they chose you
- One clear job: "Create your first event" with screenshot
- Link to 2-min video OR 3-step walkthrough (visual, not prose)
- Reassure: "Don't worry about getting it perfect"
- CTA: Button to app + optional "See how others use it" secondary link
- Tone: Energetic but calm

**Why:** Activation beats information. People need to *do* something immediately.

---

## Email 2: Show the Real Value (Day 3-4)
**Subject:** [Their Name], here's what most teams do after day 1

- Acknowledge they've tried it (if data shows usage) OR invite them to try a second feature
- Show 1-2 "next-level" use cases relevant to their signup info (solo user vs. team, etc.)
- Feature highlight: Something that reduces friction they probably experienced
  - E.g., "Sharing a calendar takes one click, not five emails"
- Social proof snippet: "87% of teams use [specific feature] within their first week"
- CTA: Either "Try [feature]" or "See how [company type] schedules smarter"

**Why:** They've survived day 1. Now show why they shouldn't leave.

---

## Email 3: Remove Barriers + Upgrade Path (Day 7-8)
**Subject:** One thing holding you back (+ how to fix it)

- Address a common sticking point based on their behavior OR segment:
  - *"Connecting your calendar"* / *"Inviting your team"* / *"Setting up notifications"*
- Troubleshoot in 3 bullets max
- Feature comparison or pricing preview (no pressure)
- Personal ask: "Reply to this email if you're stuck. We read every one."
- CTA: "Upgrade now" + "Book a 15-min demo" (if appropriate for their plan)

**Why:** Trial conversion happens when blockers are solved. Make it easy to say yes.

---

## Skeleton Notes

- **Segment by signup type** (solo/team/department) to customize features
- **Track email opens + app engagement** — adjust send times/content based on activity
- **Don't oversell** — you've got them for 14 days usually; use it to build habit, not load them with features
- **Make help accessible** — every email should have an escape hatch (link to docs, support, or reply-to-us)
