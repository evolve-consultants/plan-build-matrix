### Three Starting Points (Ordered by Caution)

| | Path | Time | What You Learn | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **A: Validate First** | Talk to 10–15 people outside your company who have the same problem. Measure their willingness to pay and use. Don't touch code yet. | 2–4 weeks | Whether there's a real market and what features matter most | Low. You find out before investing. |
| **B: Soft Launch** | License your current tool to 1–2 friendly customers (for free or cheap). Learn what breaks when someone else uses it. | 2–3 months | What documentation, support, and hardening you'll actually need | Medium. You learn in a controlled way. |
| **C: Product Sprint** | Commit a focused 4–8 week sprint to cleaning up the codebase, packaging it, and building minimal marketing. Then launch. | 2–3 months | Whether you can ship something polished and whether the market shows up | Highest. You invest before validating. |

### What Each Tells You

- **Path A** answers: "Is this a real business opportunity?" If no one will pay, you save months.
- **Path B** answers: "Can we actually deliver this to external customers?" This is where most products die — not in the market, but in operations.
- **Path C** answers: "Can we compete?" But only if you already know the answers to A and B.

### Recommendation
**Start with Path A (Validate First)**, because:
1. You'll talk to your actual market and learn what you don't know yet
2. If it's real, you'll have evidence to justify investing in Path B
3. If it's not real, you've only spent 4 weeks instead of 6 months
4. The conversations themselves generate leads and credibility

If after 10 interviews you have 2–3 people who say "I would absolutely buy/use this," you've earned the right to move to Path B.
