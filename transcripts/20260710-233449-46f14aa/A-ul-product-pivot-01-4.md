# Starting Point Questions

Before moving code, get clear on:

**Market reality**
- Who specifically would buy this? (Not "companies that do X" — actual personas)
- Would they pay for it, or just use free alternatives?
- How do you know? (Have you asked them?)

**Your actual advantage**
- What makes yours different from existing tools?
- Is it genuinely better, or just familiar because you built it?
- Could someone replicate it in a few months?

**The brutal economics**
- Internal tool = built for your workflow, possibly with technical debt
- Product = needs docs, support, reliability, upgrades, security patches
- This often costs 3-5x the original development

# Concrete First Steps

1. **Talk to 10-15 potential customers** before touching code. Ask:
   - Would they use it?
   - What would they pay?
   - What problems does it solve for them?
   - How painful is their current solution?

2. **Do a ruthless audit** of your tool:
   - Remove internal-only features
   - Document hidden assumptions
   - Estimate cleanup work
   - Check for security/compliance issues

3. **Start small**: Can you validate demand with a landing page and email list before committing?

**The question that matters most:** Are you building this because customers want it, or because you want to build a product? Those have very different success rates.

What's drawing you to productize this specifically?
