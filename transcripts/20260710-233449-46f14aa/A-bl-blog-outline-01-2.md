# Why We Moved Away From Microservices

## I. Introduction
- Hook: The appeal of microservices vs. the messy reality
- Thesis: Our transition back to monolith/modular approach

## II. The Initial Appeal
- Why we adopted microservices in the first place
- Expected benefits (scalability, team autonomy, independent deployment)

## III. Operational Complexity
- Deployment and orchestration overhead
- Monitoring, debugging, and observability challenges
- Infrastructure costs

## IV. Development Velocity Slowdown
- Network latency and cascading failures
- Complexity of testing across services
- Coordination overhead between teams

## V. Data Consistency & Integration Issues
- Distributed transaction problems
- Data duplication and sync challenges
- Schema management across services

## VI. Hidden Costs We Didn't Anticipate
- Team scaling requirements
- Tooling and infrastructure investment
- Learning curve and knowledge silos

## VII. What Actually Worked Better
- Our alternative approach (monolith, modular, strangler pattern, etc.)
- Concrete improvements (speed, reliability, cost)

## VIII. Conclusion
- When microservices make sense vs. when they don't
- Lessons learned for readers considering the same move
