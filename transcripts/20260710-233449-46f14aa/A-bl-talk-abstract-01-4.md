# Design System Talk Abstract [Draft]

Here's a structure that tends to land well for these talks:

---

**[HOOK]**
We built a design system because [specific pain point—not "to scale"]. Here's what we didn't expect to learn.

**[PROBLEM]**
[2-3 sentences on the actual mess you were in. Be specific enough people recognize themselves.]

**[APPROACH]**
Instead of [common wrong move], we [your actual decision].

**[PAYOFF]**
The result: [concrete outcome]. But more importantly: [what it taught us about design/org/process].

**[AUDIENCE TAKE-HOME]**
If you're considering this, [one key insight that applies beyond your specific context].

---

**Why this works:**
- Opens with specificity (not platitudes)
- Shows honest decision-making, not a victory lap
- Ends with something they can actually use
- Implies there was friction worth hearing about
