# Design System Talk Abstract Template

Here's a structure that tends to land well at conferences:

---

## Hook + Stakes
Start with a relatable problem or moment of friction—not the solution yet. What was broken before you built this?

*Example: "Six months in, our product teams were shipping the same button component six different ways."*

## The Journey (not just the destination)
Audiences connect with process more than outcomes. What's one counterintuitive decision or unexpected obstacle?

*"We thought we'd start with components. We actually needed to start with principles."*

## The Insight
One clear thing you learned that others might apply. This is your real value to the room.

*"Adoption isn't about documentation—it's about making it easier to use the system than not to."*

## Concrete Proof
One specific metric or before/after that shows it worked.

---

## Shape Tips
- **Open with tension**, not credentials
- **Lead with "we learned X"** rather than "we built X"
- Keep it under 150 words if possible
- End with a question that makes people think, not a mic drop

---

What's the biggest surprise or lesson from your process? That's usually your best opening.
