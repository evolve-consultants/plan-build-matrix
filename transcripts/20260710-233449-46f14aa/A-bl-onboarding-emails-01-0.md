# Trial Onboarding Sequence

## Email 1: Welcome + First Win (Send: Day 0, immediately after signup)

**Subject:** You're in—let's get your first event scheduled

**Goal:** Quick activation. Reduce blank-slate paralysis.

**Structure:**
- Warm welcome (2-3 lines)
- One obvious next step: "Create your first event in 60 seconds" (link to in-app template or guided flow)
- Screenshot or GIF showing the simplest happy path
- Reassurance: "No credit card required. Your trial lasts [X] days."
- One helpful resource link (knowledge base article or video walkthrough)
- Optional: social proof (e.g., "Teams are already saving X hours/week")

**Tone:** Friendly, uncluttered. Make them feel capable.

---

## Email 2: Show Depth (Send: Day 3–4, after they've used it once)

**Subject:** Here's what power users do with [app name]

**Goal:** Prevent feature discovery fatigue. Plant seeds for stickier workflows.

**Structure:**
- Acknowledge what they've already done ("Nice—we saw you created your first event")
- Highlight 2–3 features that match their use case (ideally based on their behavior: team vs. solo, recurring events, integrations, etc.)
- Use concrete language: "Slack reminders mean your team never forgets" vs. "seamless integrations"
- Link to a short walkthrough or case study for each feature
- Subtle scarcity: "Time-blocking templates save [X] hours in setup"

**Tone:** Helpful guide, not aggressive. You're expanding their mental model of what's possible.

---

## Email 3: Conversion + Unblock (Send: Day 8–10, before trial ends)

**Subject:** One thing holding you back? We can fix it. [+gentle deadline]

**Goal:** Remove friction before the ask. Convert or gracefully extend.

**Structure:**
- State the offer clearly: "Your trial ends in [X] days"
- Open loop: "What's one thing that would make this a no-brainer?" + link to 2-min feedback form
- Alternative: if they haven't used a key feature, offer a **direct unblock** ("Want to connect your calendar? Here's a 3-min guide" or "Scheduling with your team? Let's set up a quick Slack integration")
- Social proof moment: link to testimonial or ROI calculator
- CTA options:
  - "Start your subscription" (confident)
  - "Chat with us" (softer, for fence-sitters)
  - "Extend trial" (if retention data suggests they need more time)

**Tone:** Respectful, problem-solver mindset. Assume they want it to work.

---

## Variation Notes

- **Adjust timing** based on your trial length (these assume 14 days)
- **Segment Email 2** if you have behavior data (teams vs. soloists get different features highlighted)
- **A/B the subject lines**—experiment with curiosity-gap vs. benefit-led
- **Email 3 CTA placement**: consider multiple CTAs at different friction levels (form → chat → subscribe)
