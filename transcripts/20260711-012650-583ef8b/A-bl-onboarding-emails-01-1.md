# Trial Onboarding Sequence

## Email 1: Welcome + Quick Win (Send immediately)
**Subject:** Your scheduling app is ready—do this first

- Brief welcome (1-2 sentences)
- One obvious first task: "Import your calendar" or "Schedule your first meeting"
- Direct link to that action
- Very short explainer of why (reduces friction, shows value fast)
- Optional: 1-minute video walkthrough link
- Keep it short—you want them *in* the product, not reading

---

## Email 2: Feature Highlight (Day 3-4)
**Subject:** [Feature name] saves our team X hours/week

- Lead with the benefit they probably haven't discovered yet
- Real example of how it works (your team or a customer)
- Screenshot or GIF showing the feature in action
- CTA: "Try [feature] in your account" (direct link)
- Anticipate a common friction point and explain around it

*Choose the feature that creates the most obvious value for your use case—probably time savings or reduced back-and-forth.*

---

## Email 3: Gentle Nudge + Support (Day 6-7)
**Subject:** Quick question: how's the trial going?

- Acknowledge they're in the trial window still
- Ask what they're using it for (builds relationship, gives you signal)
- 2-3 resources: help docs, feature guide, or comparison to what they used before
- Low-pressure CTA: upgrade link + "questions?" offer
- Optional: discount or deadline if that fits your model

---

**Variables to shape:**
- When does value show up in *your* product? (Day 1? After they invite a team?)
- What feature makes people go "oh, *that's* why this is useful"?
- What's the real sticking point in onboarding? (Integrate, invite team, etc.)
