# Key starting points

**Validate there's actually a market**
- Are clients asking to buy this separately? That's your strongest signal
- Talk to 10-15 potential customers (not your existing clients) about their pain point and willingness to pay
- Check if similar products exist and how they're priced

**Assess your actual advantage**
- Is this genuinely better than alternatives, or just better *for you*?
- Can you maintain it as a separate product, or is it too tied to your consulting way of doing things?
- Do you have defensible IP, or will competitors copy it quickly?

**Understand the business model shift**
- Selling software is very different from consulting (different sales cycles, support model, margins, churn dynamics)
- You'd likely need to choose: stay consulting-focused with a small product, or pivot to product
- Running both well simultaneously is surprisingly hard

**Start small & reversible**
- Could you sell it as a standalone tool to a few consulting clients first?
- This tells you if people actually value it without the full product infrastructure

**The hard questions**
- Do you *want* to run a product business? (Longer sales cycles, continuous development, support obligations)
- Or would licensing/white-labeling to competitors be better for your situation?

What's your sense of demand so far—are clients already asking about this, or is it more that you think it could be valuable?
