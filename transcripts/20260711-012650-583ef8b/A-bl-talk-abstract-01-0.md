# Design System Conference Talk Abstract

## Here's a strong skeleton to populate:

**[COMPELLING OPENING]**
We built a design system that actually got used. Here's what we did differently.

**[THE PROBLEM]**
[Your starting point: chaos, duplication, brand drift, developer-designer friction—pick the real pain]

**[THE APPROACH]**
Rather than [common mistake], we [your key decision]. This meant [one concrete example of how that played out].

**[THE INSIGHT]**
The breakthrough came when we realized [the non-obvious thing you learned—this is your talk's real value].

**[WHAT CHANGED]**
By [specific mechanism: governance model, tooling choice, ritual you created], we moved from [metric/state A] to [metric/state B].

**[WHO SHOULD LISTEN]**
You'll leave with [one concrete takeaway relevant to: designers building systems / engineers maintaining them / leaders funding them—pick your crowd].

---

## Punching it up:

- Lead with your **boldest true claim** (not "we built a system" but "our system reduced handoff time by X" or "it actually got adopted")
- Cut anything that sounds like a victory lap—audiences want obstacles and honest missteps
- End on *their* problem, not your solution

What's the real problem you solved, and what surprised you most in solving it?
