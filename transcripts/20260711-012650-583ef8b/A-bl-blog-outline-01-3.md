# Outline: Why We Moved Away From Microservices

## Opening
- Hook: The hype vs. reality gap
- Brief context: When/why you adopted microservices
- Thesis: What you learned that changed your mind

## The Operational Burden
- Deployment complexity and tooling overhead
- Monitoring, logging, and debugging across services
- Infrastructure costs (didn't shrink like expected)
- Team size needed to manage it all

## Hidden Complexity
- Distributed systems problems (eventual consistency, cascading failures)
- Network calls replacing simple function calls
- Data consistency nightmares
- Testing became exponentially harder

## Developer Experience
- Slower local development setup
- Increased cognitive load
- Onboarding friction
- Harder to trace bugs across services

## What Actually Worked Better
- Modular monolith alternative you chose
- Cleaner boundaries without distribution
- Specific wins you saw post-migration

## Lessons & Nuance
- Microservices aren't inherently bad
- Conditions where they actually make sense
- What you'd do differently
