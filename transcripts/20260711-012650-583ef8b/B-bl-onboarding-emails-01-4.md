## Why this structure

- **Email 1** removes activation friction — one action, instant feedback
- **Email 2** solves a problem they'll actually hit (not a nice-to-have)
- **Email 3** acknowledges their progress, names a second pain point, introduces pricing before trial ends

Each one has a clear next action and assumes they've used the product (not just signed up).

## What I'd adjust based on:
- **Trial length** — if it's 7 days, compress email 2 and 3. If it's 30, you could space these further and add a 4th.
- **Your hook** — if the core value is "team alignment," reframe each email around that. If it's "no-show reduction," lead with that problem.
- **Tone** — this is casual/direct. Some products need formal or playful instead.
