# Key Starting Questions

**Validate there's actually a market:**
- Did clients ever ask to buy this tool separately?
- Would they pay for it, or just use it if you offered it free?
- Are there obvious competitors, and if so, why would yours win?

**Understand what you actually have:**
- Is it genuinely generalizable, or deeply baked into your consulting workflows?
- How much work to make it usable by strangers vs. your team?
- Does it depend on your consulting brand/expertise to have value?

**Reality-check the effort:**
- Product work (support, documentation, hosting, security) often takes 3x the engineering
- Can you sustain this alongside consulting, or does it need focus?
- What's the honest revenue potential vs. the distraction cost?

# Honest next steps

1. **Talk to 5-10 potential customers** (not your current clients—they're biased) about whether they'd actually buy
2. **Estimate the rewrite burden** - code for internal use is usually rough
3. **Do the math** - what pricing would make this worth your time? Does that seem realistic?

The graveyard is full of "we should productize this" ideas that looked better in theory. The ones that work usually had specific customers already interested before day one.

What type of tool are we talking about? That changes the equation quite a bit.
