# Starting Point: Reality Check First

Before investing heavily, validate that others actually want this:

**Quick tests (low effort):**
- Talk to 5-10 people in your target market about their pain point. Do they describe the problem unprompted? Would they pay?
- Check if competitors exist. If none, that's often a red flag (not a gap, but no market)
- Search job boards/Reddit/Twitter for people complaining about this specific problem
- Look at TAM realistically—is it big enough to matter?

If those conversations go well, *then*:

# Practical Next Steps

1. **Extract the MVP ruthlessly**
   - What's the smallest version that solves the core problem?
   - Strip out customizations you built for yourself
   - Document what you cut—you'll add it back later if customers want it

2. **Understand your actual moat**
   - Why did you build it better than alternatives?
   - Is it defensible, or just a temporary advantage?
   - (This affects pricing and positioning)

3. **Pick a business model**
   - SaaS, open-source + paid tier, standalone license, API?
   - Your choice here determines everything downstream

4. **Get customers (don't build in isolation)**
   - Launch rough. Talk to users constantly.
   - You'll learn your product is solving the wrong problem or needs repositioning—better to learn this early
