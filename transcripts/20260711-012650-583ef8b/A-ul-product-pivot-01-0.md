Here's a practical sequence:

**Validate there's actual demand (before building anything)**
- Talk to 10-15 people in your target market, not clients. Ask what they'd pay, not if they'd use it
- Check if competitors exist and how they're doing
- Be honest: is this solving a real problem or just scratching your own itch?

**Assess what you actually have**
- How much of your tooling is genuinely novel vs. standard practice?
- Could someone replicate it in a weekend or does it have real defensibility?
- Is it deeply tied to your consultancy's specific workflows (bad for productizing)?

**Start minimal**
- Don't rebuild it for external users yet
- Pick your narrowest viable customer segment
- Make a simple landing page, collect emails, validate willingness to pay
- Consider selling it as a service (you run it) before a self-serve product

**Understand the shift**
- Consulting margins ≠ SaaS margins (you'll need way higher volume or pricing)
- You'll spend less time on the tool than on support, sales, and keeping users happy
- Your consultancy and product compete for your attention

**Key question to ask yourself:**
Do you want to build a product business or monetize existing IP while keeping your consultancy? These have very different requirements.
