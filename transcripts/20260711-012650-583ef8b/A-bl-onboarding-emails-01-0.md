# 3-Email Trial Onboarding Sequence

## Email 1: Welcome + Quick Win (Day 0)
**Subject:** Your scheduling app is ready—here's the 2-minute setup

**Goal:** Get them into the product immediately

- Brief welcome (1-2 sentences)
- **One specific action:** "Create your first event in 3 clicks" with a direct link
- Show a single screenshot of the core feature
- Social proof: "X teams scheduled their first meeting today"
- CTA: "Start scheduling" (not generic "learn more")
- Optional: Offer a 5-min setup call if they want it

**Tone:** Eager but not pushy

---

## Email 2: Show the Value (Day 3-4)
**Subject:** See how [Company Name] saved 4 hours on scheduling this week

**Goal:** Demonstrate ROI before they forget about the trial

- Lead with a concrete use case relevant to their industry/company size
- Feature highlight: One thing they probably haven't discovered yet (sync, automation, team views—whatever solves a real pain)
- Include a short video (30 sec) or GIF showing that feature in action
- Ask one diagnostic question: "What's your biggest scheduling bottleneck?" (captures intent without being salesy)
- CTA: "See how it works" or "Show me more"

**Tone:** Helpful consultant

---

## Email 3: Last Chance + Decision Path (Day 10-12)
**Subject:** Your trial ends soon—here's what to choose

**Goal:** Push decision-making; separate converts from tire-kickers

- Acknowledge the trial endpoint directly
- **3 clear paths:**
  - "Not for us right now" → feedback form (salvage data, stay in orbit)
  - "Need more time" → extend button (removes friction)
  - "Let's do this" → pricing/plan selector (frictionless upgrade)
- Include one customer quote or case result
- If appropriate: time-limited incentive (first month off, etc.)
- CTA: Multiple buttons or links for each path

**Tone:** Direct but not desperate

---

## Notes to Shape:
- What's your actual trial length?
- Do you have product tours/onboarding in-app already?
- What's the #1 feature that converts people?
- Who's the buyer vs. user (affects messaging)?
- Do you have customer data on when people typically churn during trial?
