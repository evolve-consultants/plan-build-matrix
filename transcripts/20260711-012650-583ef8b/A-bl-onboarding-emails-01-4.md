# Trial Onboarding Sequence

## Email 1: Welcome + Quick Win (Day 0)
**Subject:** You're in—here's your first 5 minutes

- Warm greeting, set expectations (14-day trial, no CC required)
- **One specific action:** "Create your first event" with a direct link
- Show a 30-second GIF of the core flow (create → invite → done)
- Reassure: "We keep this simple on purpose"
- Footer: link to help docs, not overwhelming

**Goal:** Get them *doing* something immediately. Activation beats education.

---

## Email 2: Show the Real Value (Day 3)
**Subject:** Here's what you're missing (if you only use the basics)

- Assume they've created an event; show the *next* thing
- Could be: automations, team visibility, timezone handling, recurring meetings—whatever moves them from "neat tool" to "solves my actual problem"
- Use a real use case: "You're coordinating with 3 people across time zones? Here's how..."
- Include a short video or screenshot showing the feature in action
- Optional: 1-2 sentence user testimonial

**Goal:** Deepen engagement. Show depth without overwhelming.

---

## Email 3: Last Chance + Social Proof (Day 12)
**Subject:** Your trial ends in 2 days—here's what people love about us

- Direct: "Your trial ends [date]"
- 3 short bullets about outcomes users see (time saved, fewer back-and-forths, etc.)
- Social proof: customer stat or quote
- Single CTA: upgrade link OR "not right now—tell us why" (feedback loop)
- Optional: limited offer (if you do them)

**Goal:** Convert or learn why they won't. Either way, you win data.
