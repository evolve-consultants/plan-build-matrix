# Starting Points

**Validate there's actually demand beyond your own use:**
- Talk to 10-15 potential customers in your space before building anything new
- Listen for: unprompted mentions of the problem, budget availability, urgency
- Be skeptical of polite interest—you want people frustrated enough to switch

**Understand what you'd actually be selling:**
- Is it the tool itself, or the specific configuration/domain expertise you've baked into it?
- What parts of your internal version are unique vs. replicating existing solutions?
- Could competitors copy this in 3-6 months?

**Get realistic about the jump:**
- Consulting and products have totally different economics, support models, and sales cycles
- You'd likely need to run both in parallel initially, which is resource-heavy
- Many consultancies that tried this found it diverted focus from their core business

**Practical next steps:**
1. Document what the tool actually does (forces clarity)
2. Show it to 5-10 people outside your firm, watch them use it
3. Ask directly: "Would you pay for this? How much? When would you need it?"
4. Only then decide if it's worth the operational shift

**Hard question:** Are you excited about building a product business, or just about maximizing an existing asset? Those require different commitments.
