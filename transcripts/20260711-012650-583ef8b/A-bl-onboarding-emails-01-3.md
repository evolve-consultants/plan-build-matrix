# Trial Onboarding Sequence

## Email 1: Welcome + Quick Win (Day 0)
**Subject:** Your free trial is live—schedule your first event in 60 seconds

**Goal:** Get them *using* it immediately, not reading docs

- Warm welcome, name drop their company if you have it
- One screenshot showing the simplest possible action (create event)
- Direct link to that feature ("Start here")
- Quick mention of what makes you different (if you have a hook—integration ecosystem? AI scheduling? Mobile-first?)
- Soft CTA to reply with questions
- Save the feature tour for later

---

## Email 2: Aha Moment Enabler (Day 2–3)
**Subject:** The feature that saves people 5+ hours/week

**Goal:** Show them the specific value, not the full feature set

- Lead with *one* powerful feature that matches their use case (if you segment by industry, use their segment)
- Concrete example: "If you have 15+ meetings/week, this cuts rescheduling time by..."
- Video walkthrough or GIF (not a 10-min demo)
- "Try it with [specific scenario]" CTA
- Social proof if you have it (testimonial, stat, case study snippet)
- Mention the catch-all: "Stuck? Reply here"

---

## Email 3: Activation + Decision (Day 5–7)
**Subject:** Before your trial ends: one thing you might've missed

**Goal:** Remove obstacles to conversion; address objections

- Acknowledge they haven't activated yet (if they haven't)—make it low-friction to circle back
- Address common friction: integrations, team setup, data import, pricing clarity
- One final win: "Here's how to get your whole team on board in 10 minutes"
- Clear next step: upgrade link, book a call, or "finish the walkthrough"
- Optional: small incentive (extended trial, discount on annual, etc.) if appropriate for your model

---

## Notes to shape this:
- **What's your core aha moment?** (The feature/benefit that clicks for most users)
- **Who are you targeting?** (Solo users? Teams? Which industry?)
- **What's your highest drop-off point?** (Usually multi-user setup or integrations)
- **Pricing/timeline?** (This changes urgency in email 3)

What's your product's core value prop, and who's your ideal trial user?
