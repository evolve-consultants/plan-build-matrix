## Draft Sequence

### Email 1: Day 1 — Welcome + First Win (Sent immediately after signup)

**Subject**: Your first event in [Product Name] — takes 30 seconds

**Body**:
- Warm welcome, acknowledge they just signed up
- One-sentence value prop focused on **speed** or **relief** (not feature count)
- Guided first step: "Create your first event in 2 clicks" with a single CTA button to the app
- Screenshot or brief copy showing how trivial this is
- Reassure: "No setup required—you're already ready"

**Goal**: Get them into the app and feeling competent in < 2 minutes. Social proof or feature list kills this.

---

### Email 2: Day 3 or 4 — Feature Unlock + Social Proof

**Subject**: Here's what your [Product Name] trial unlocks

**Body**:
- Acknowledge they've had time to try it ("You've probably noticed...")
- Surface 2–3 core features that solve real friction in their workflow (team visibility, conflict detection, integration X, etc.—pick the ones that turn trial into habit)
- Each feature gets a short benefit statement + one screenshot or animated gif
- Include a single testimonial or metric (e.g., "Teams using [feature] save 3 hours/week")
- CTA: link to in-app feature tour OR a specific workflow tutorial

**Goal**: Move from "I tried it" to "I see how this saves me time." Feature-focused but benefit-led.

---

### Email 3: Day 10–12 (before trial end) — Urgency + Conversion

**Subject**: Your [Product Name] trial ends in [X] days. Here's what's next.

**Body**:
- Direct: trial is ending
- Reflect back one thing they've done ("We noticed you created X events..."), if your data supports it. Otherwise: "You've seen how much faster scheduling gets."
- 2–3 sentence value reminder (not a feature dump)
- Clear comparison table or simple pricing (don't hide it)
- Two buttons: "Start paid plan" (primary) and "Need more time?" (secondary—link to extend trial if you offer it, or sales chat)
- Optional: small discount or incentive if conversion is important

**Goal**: Decide, don't disappear. Make the next step obvious.

---

## Why this structure

- **Email 1** lowers activation energy (competence matters more than features)
- **Email 2** builds habit (shows depth without overwhelming)
- **Email 3** acknowledges the trial end and removes friction from the conversion decision
- **Tone throughout**: conversational, benefit-focused, not salesy
