# Trial Onboarding Sequence

## Email 1: Welcome + Quick Win (Day 1)
**Subject:** You're in—let's schedule your first meeting in 60 seconds

**Goal:** Lower activation barrier, build immediate confidence

- Warm greeting + what they got access to
- *One* screenshot or 30-second video showing the fastest path to their first scheduled meeting
- Direct link to that feature (not general dashboard)
- Short paragraph on what makes your approach different (speed, integrations, whatever)
- CTA: "Schedule your first meeting" + link
- Soft secondary: "Questions? Reply to this email"

---

## Email 2: Show Real Value (Day 4-5)
**Subject:** [Name], here's what you might be missing

**Goal:** Demonstrate depth, prevent "nice tool but..." abandonment

- Reference their actual usage (if they've logged in: "We saw you created X meetings"; if not: adjust tone)
- 2-3 specific features/benefits tailored to *their* use case (infer from signup info if available: solo vs. team, industry, etc.)
- Social proof relevant to their segment ("Teams at [industry] save 8 hours/month")
- One concrete example scenario ("If you schedule 15+ meetings weekly, here's...")
- CTA: Link to a specific feature tour or knowledge base article
- Escape hatch: "Not quite what you need? Let's talk" (way to salvage)

---

## Email 3: Conversion Push + Urgency (Day 10)
**Subject:** Your trial ends in 4 days—[specific thing they'd lose]

**Goal:** Convert or gather feedback for future re-engagement

- Clear trial expiration date + what happens
- Remind them of their biggest "aha" moment from trial (successful meetings created, time saved, etc.)
- Limited-time offer (if you have one): discount, feature unlock, or extended trial
- If they haven't used it much: alternative CTA—"Let's find the right fit" (demo, consultation, downgrade to freemium)
- Final CTA: "Upgrade now" + secondary "I need more time"

---

## Notes to shape:
- **Personalization hooks:** What do you know at signup that could segment these three emails? (Team size, use case, industry)
- **Tone:** Is this playful or professional?
- **Your actual differentiation:** What would make Email 2's value prop land harder?
- **Fallback strategy:** What's your move if they're a bad fit? (Freemium tier? Feedback loop?)

What does your product's main "aha moment" tend to be?
