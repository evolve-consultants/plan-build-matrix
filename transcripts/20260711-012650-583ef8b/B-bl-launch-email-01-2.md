### Why this structure works

- **Subject is plain.** No hype verb. "Now has" is confident without being loud.
- **First line is the fact.** Users decode it in half a second; everything after explains why they should care.
- **Second paragraph speaks to outcome, not feature.** "No more back-and-forth" is what matters. The feature (booking) is already stated.
- **Availability is explicit.** Removes the "is this for me?" friction.
- **Next steps are single-click.** One CTA, one fallback, done.
- **Tone is matter-of-fact.** Confidence comes from knowing it works and stating it simply, not from salesiness.

### What might need adjusting

- **Use case and tier language** — I've left these as placeholders. The specificity here is where your product knowledge comes in.
- **Length** — This is tight. If you need to justify the feature more (why now, competitive advantage, customer request), that usually belongs in a follow-up or a longer version for announcement channels. This version assumes the feature speaks for itself.
- **Personalization level** — This reads as a segment-of-one or small group message. If it's going to hundreds, you might add a subject-line variable or a brief context paragraph up top.
