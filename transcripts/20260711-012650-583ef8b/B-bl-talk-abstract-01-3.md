### Draft Abstract

**How We Built a Design System (Without Losing Our Minds)**

Every company at scale hits the same wall: inconsistent UI, duplicated components, designers and engineers speaking different languages. We were no exception.

This talk is the story of how we went from a fragmented pile of one-off solutions to a shared, living design system—and what we learned along the way that surprised us.

We'll cover the unglamorous part: how we got buy-in from skeptical teams, what we got wrong in the first version, and why governance matters more than you think. But mostly, we'll talk about the structural decisions that actually stuck—the ones that survived contact with real products and real deadlines.

If you're building a design system, considering one, or trapped inside one that isn't working, you'll leave with a map: what to do first, what to leave for later, and how to know if you're solving the right problem.

### Why this shape works
- **Opens with pain**: signals immediately that this is about a real problem
- **Reframes the talk**: not "look how perfect we are," but "here's what we learned"
- **Promises specifics without spoiling them**: "unglamorous part," "structural decisions," "what we got wrong"
- **Closes with utility**: gives the audience three concrete takeaways to expect
- **Tone**: pragmatic and honest, which builds trust and filters for the right listeners
