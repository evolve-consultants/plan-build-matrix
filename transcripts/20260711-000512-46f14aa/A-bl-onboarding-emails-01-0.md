# 3-Email Onboarding Sequence: Scheduling App Trial

Here's a rough structure to shape with your specifics:

---

**Email 1: Day 0 — "Get your first win fast"**
*Trigger: immediately on signup*

- Goal: get them to complete ONE core action (probably: connect calendar + create first scheduling link)
- Skip the feature tour — just point at the single next step
- Subject line idea: something task-oriented, not "Welcome!" (e.g. "Set up your first booking link (2 min)")
- Include a short line addressing the obvious hesitation (e.g. calendar permissions/privacy)
- CTA: one button, one action

---

**Email 2: Day 2–3 — "Here's what changes once it clicks"**
*Trigger: time-based, OR behavior-based if they haven't done the Email 1 action yet*

- Branch this if you can:
  - **If they completed setup:** show them a second, slightly more advanced feature (buffer times, team scheduling, integrations — whatever creates stickiness)
  - **If they didn't:** re-send the nudge from Email 1 with a different angle (social proof, a common blocker removed, or a shorter version of the task)
- Goal: deepen usage or resurrect stalled activation
- Consider a lightweight customer example/quote here — real use case > feature list

---

**Email 3: Day 5–6 — "Trial's ending, here's the decision"**
*Trigger: 2-3 days before trial expiry*

- Goal: convert or get explicit feedback
- Structure:
  1. Quick reminder of what they've set up / used so far (personalize with actual usage data if you can pull it)
  2. Clear statement of what happens at trial end + pricing link
  3. Low-pressure out: "Not the right fit? Reply and tell us why" — this is gold for you even if they don't convert
- Avoid discount-first — lead with value recap, offer help second

---

### Things to decide before writing final copy:
1. **Do you have behavior-tracking to branch Email 2 and 3?** If not, I'd design 3B as a fallback path.
2. **What's the single "aha" action** that correlates most with conversion? Everything hinges on that.
3. **Tone** — how formal is your brand? I wrote this neutral/direct; can shift.
