# Building a Design System Without Losing Our Minds (Or Our Brand)

**Abstract**

Every design system starts with the best intentions and a Figma file. Ours started the same way—then promptly collided with six product teams, three engineering stacks, and a brand guideline PDF that hadn't been updated since before half our components existed.

This talk is the story of how we built a design system that actually gets used: not the polished retrospective where everything goes according to plan, but the real one, with the false starts, the components nobody adopted, and the governance model we had to scrap after month two.

I'll walk through the decisions that mattered more than we expected—how we chose what to systematize first, why "consistency" isn't actually the goal, and how we got designers and engineers to stop treating the design system as someone else's job. Along the way: the tooling choices that paid off, the ones that didn't, and the cultural shifts that turned out to matter more than any component library.

You'll leave with a clearer sense of what a design system is actually for, a few hard-won opinions about process and ownership, and permission to skip the parts of the "official" playbook that don't fit your team.
