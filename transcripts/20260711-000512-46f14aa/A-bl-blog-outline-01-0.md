# Blog Post Outline: Why We Moved Away from Microservices

## Title Options
- "Why We Ditched Microservices (And You Might Want To Too)"
- "Our Microservices Journey: A Retreat to Simplicity"
- "The Monolith Strikes Back: Our Path Away from Microservices"

## Suggested Structure

**1. Hook/Intro**
- Brief statement of the counterintuitive nature (everyone's moving TO microservices, we moved away)
- One-sentence teaser of the outcome/result

**2. Context: Why We Adopted Microservices Originally**
- What problem were we trying to solve?
- Team size/structure at the time
- What was the expected win?

**3. The Cracks Started Showing**
- Specific pain points (pick 3-4 concrete examples):
  - Operational complexity (deployment, monitoring, debugging across services)
  - Team size vs. service count mismatch
  - Network latency/reliability issues
  - Data consistency headaches
  - Increased cognitive overhead for developers
  - Cost (infrastructure, tooling, headcount to manage it all)

**4. The Breaking Point**
- Was there a specific incident or realization that triggered the decision?
- Who made the call and how?

**5. The Migration/Consolidation Process**
- How did you approach moving back?
- Big bang vs. incremental?
- What did you keep as separate services vs. what got merged?
- Timeline

**6. Results**
- Concrete metrics if you have them (deploy time, incident count, dev velocity)
- Team morale/experience changes
- Cost savings

**7. Nuance/Caveats**
- This isn't "microservices are bad"—when do they still make sense?
- What size/stage of company should still consider them?
- What would make you reconsider?

**8. Key Takeaways**
- 3-5 bullet points of lessons learned
- Advice for teams considering either direction

---

**Questions to help fill this in:**
- What's your company size/stage (helps set context)?
- Do you have concrete metrics/war stories, or more qualitative experience?
- Is this technical audience or broader/business audience?
- Any specific incident that was the "last straw"?
